The Mountain Region

The Mountain Region is in california, is mainly in the Sierra Neveda Range. It has the talleset peaks in the United States of California , like Mount Whitney. Mt. Shasta and Mt Lassen are two extint volcanos. Climate in the mountains, summers are mild and winters are cold and snowy. Very few people live in the mountains because it is very snowy and these mountains are also not easily acsessible. Very high up in Sierra Nevada, small towns are found near huge trees like Redwoods, Bristlecone Pine and Douglas firs. Some of these are the oldest living trees estimated over 4,000 years old. Wild life like Bears, deer, and Mountain Lions made their homes in the mountains. Mountain recreational activities are like skiing, hiking, climbing, biking, golfing, and horse riding. The landmarks in the mountains are Yosemite Parks and Lake Tahoe. A lot of visitors visit year long.

The Desert Region

The Desert region is in the southern part of California. The desert is a dry area that gets less than 10 inches of rain each year. Climate is hot and dry year long but it is often cool at nights. In winters, the temperature can go below 0 degrees F. The highest temperature was recorded in California’s Death Valley when the temperature reached 134 degrees F in 1913. The plants in desert are Cacti, Bushes, and Sage brushes and animals are Coyotes, Lizards, and Diamondback snakes. The main landmarks are Death Valley National Park, Red Rock Canyon State Park and Joshua Tree National Park.

The Valley Region

The valley region is a flat area of land that stretches for miles between the coast ranges and Sierra Nevada. Many farms are located in the valley. Climate in the valley in winters is cold and humid but the summers are hot and dry. The valley region in California is one of the country’s best farming area because the region has rich soil and water, so the main industries in the region are agriculture and dairy. The fruits and vegetables grown in the valley region provide crops for the canning industries. The main cities and attractions in the valley region are Sacramento, Bakersfield, and Fresno.

The Coast Region

California has very long coast which is the land along an Ocean in the western side of California. Climate is mild summers and wet winters. Main cities in the coast region are San Francisco, Los Angeles and San Diego. The main industries are fishing, technology, entertainment, and tourism. The coast is densely populated. Animals like bobcats, weasels, coyotes, gray foxes, and pumas live in the coast. Sea Lions, Harbor seals, elephant seals, gray whales, sea otters, and some sharks were found in California’s coastline. The coast has so many attractions like Golden Gate Bridge, Santa Cruz board walk, 17 Mile Drive and Big Sur.
